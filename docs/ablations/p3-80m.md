# Phase 3: 77M scale-up — does scale help? (2026-06-20)

GPU scale-up on a rented ThunderCompute A6000 (per
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-20-chess-rl-phase3-gpu-scaleup-design.md`). The
question: **does a 4× bigger model, trained on the identical data, actually play
better with search?** Answered with a clean controlled experiment — a 77M model
vs a 10M control, both trained on the **same ~100M dense positions**, compared
**head-to-head at equal MCTS budget** (Phase-2's lesson: games, not puzzles, are
the strength metric).

## Setup

Both models trained on the **same** ~100M dense positions (`prdev/chessbench-full-policy-value`,
T=0.1; 100,271,212 train / 201,045 val), **25,000 steps**, batch 1024, EMA 0.999,
bf16 AMP, on one A6000. One pass over the data (zero repeats) via the new
streaming dataloader (the eager loader OOMs at this scale).

| model | preset | params | dims | lr |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **77M** | `p3-80m` | 76,982,212 | d512 / 16L / 16h / d_ff3072, SwiGLU, HL-Gauss-64, dropout 0.05 | 2e-4 |
| **10M control** | `p2-value-swiglu-drop` | 10,250,052 | d256 / 8L / 8h / d_ff1024, SwiGLU, HL-Gauss-64, dropout 0.05 | 3e-4 |

## Primary result — head-to-head (the scale test)

`arena_eval.py --vs`, MCTS 50 sims, 60 games from 8-ply random openings
(deterministic engines need opening diversity), colour-balanced:

> **77M vs 10M control: 19W / 39D / 2L → score 0.642, ≈ +101 Elo.**

Decisive games **19–2** to the 77M; only 2 losses in 60. **Scale helps in actual
play.** The bigger model, on identical data, is meaningfully stronger with search.

## Training metrics (final, step 25k, on the 100M dense val)

| model | val_policy_loss | val_top1 | value sign-acc | train wall |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 77M | **2.762** | **0.493** | **0.934** | 6.6 h |
| 10M control | 2.787 | 0.449 | 0.926 | 1.6 h |

On **general (dense) positions** the 77M leads on both policy (0.493 vs 0.449)
and value (0.934 vs 0.926). Consistent with the head-to-head: scale helps.

## Puzzle gates (`ablate.py --fast`, tactical Lichess puzzles)

| model | raw_top1@300 | raw_top1@1000 | mate-in-1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 77M | 0.290 | 0.320 | **3/3** |
| 10M control | 0.300 | 0.328 | 2/3 |
| *(ref) Phase-2 p2-value (10M, 300k data)* | — | *0.375* | *2/3* |
| *(ref) baseline-v1* | — | *0.289* | *2/3* |

**On tactical puzzles the 77M is NOT better than the 10M (0.32 vs 0.328), and
both are below Phase-2's p2-value (0.375).** Yet the 77M wins the head-to-head and
leads on dense val. This re-confirms the Phase-2 finding: **puzzle top-1 misjudges
engine strength.** The puzzle dip vs Phase-2 most likely reflects the **one-pass
over-100M regime** (vs many epochs over 300k, which over-fits the narrow puzzle
slice) plus the known **`value_loss_weight=1.0` throttle** on policy (the
64-bucket HL-Gauss CE dominates the loss). The 77M does win mate-in-1 (3/3).

## Verdict

**The primary gate passes: scale helps.** 77M beats the 10M control +101 Elo
head-to-head at equal MCTS, on identical data — a clean, real result at our
budget. It shows up in *general play* (dense val) and *games*, not in *tactical
puzzles*, exactly as the puzzles-aren't-strength lesson predicts.

This does **not** mean we're near Stockfish — it means the architecture+recipe
scales, which is the question Phase 3 was built to answer. Honest ceiling
unchanged: a strong neural engine at ~1 epoch over 100M, not a teacher-beater
(that needs the self-play loop + far more data; see
`docs/codex_comments/sota_elo_architecture_recommendations.md`).

## Caveats & next

- **Data/compute-bound, not capacity-bound.** 25k steps ≈ 1 pass over 100M (25M
  positions of compute). Val loss had flattened but the model is under-trained
  for its size — more steps / more data is the lever (snapshot `chess-rl-p3-100m-data`
  preserves the 100M + env for a longer run).
- **`value_loss_weight=0.3` experiment** (warm-start from `p3_80m/best_ema.pt`):
  the clean A/B to test whether down-weighting value recovers policy top-1
  without losing the value head that drives MCTS. Deferred follow-up.
- **Pending (slow on local MPS, optional):** absolute-Elo Stockfish ladder
  (skills ≥6) and the secondary head-to-head vs the prior champion
  `p2_full_300k` (confounded by data, hence secondary).
- Value calibration (expected-score buckets) appended below once computed.
